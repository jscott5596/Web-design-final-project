Paycor Stadium

This is image comes from City Beat

The home of the bengals, aswell as the unofficial largest concert venue in Cincinnati, Paycor stadium has become one of Cincinnati's most identifable landmarks. Prior to the 2022 season, the stadium was named after the legendary founder of both the Bengals and Browns-Paul Brown. The stadium currently has a seating capacity of just north of 65,000 seats, though in recent years the Bengals have used over flow seating to adjust to the increasing crowd. Nicknamed "the jungle", you will certainly find a loyal and roaring crowd no matter the time of year or event.

*Benagls Fight Song for your enjoyment

Nippert Stadium

This is image comes from Go Bearcats.com

Being originally built all the way back in 1915, the stadium has been renovated several times since then, but still retains several historical parts. Nippert Stadium is home to the UC bearcats football program, and is one of the older fields in college football. The stadium was named after UC player James "Jimmy" Nippert who died due to infection he sustained during a game. On a less gruesome note, Nippert has an approximate seating capcity of 40,000 seats, but can be expanded if need be.

Great American Ball Park

This is image comes from Wikipedia

Located in the banks, and right next to Paycor Stadium, Great American Ball Park is a beloved landmark for baseball fans across the country. Since its opening in 2003, it has served as the home of the Cincinnati Reds and has hosted numerous games and events, including the MLB All-Star Game in 2015. The stadium boasts a seating capacity of over 42,000, with the iconic Riverfront bleachers offering stunning views of the Ohio River. Great American Ball Park is known for its state-of-the-art amenities, including its high-definition video board and immersive sound system. Whether you're a die-hard baseball fan or simply looking for a fun day out with family and friends, Great American Ball Park is the place to be.

TQL Stadium

This is image comes from fccincinnati.com

Though it was only finished in 2020, TQL stadium has quickly become a destination site. Located in the west end, it boasts a seating capcity of 25,500, though seating can be expanded. Given FC cincinnati's recent success good luck actually finding a seat, but assuming you do you are in for an experience. The crowd is known to be loyal and fun-lovingin a way that is reminiscent of the Bengals, but also the soccer crowds of Europe. Even if you aren't looking to catch a soccer game, TQL stadium is designed to be a year-round venue with it hosting several concerts and other events.

Heritage Bank Center

This is image comes from heritagebankcenter.com

Located in the heart of downtown Cincinnati, the Heritage Bank Center, formerly known as the US Bank Arena, is a premier venue for sports, entertainment, and special events. The center has hosted a wide range of events, including ice hockey, basketball, wrestling, and concerts by some of the biggest names in music. With a seating capacity of over 17,000, the arena offers a unique and intimate atmosphere for fans to enjoy their favorite events. The venue has undergone several renovations in recent years to enhance the fan experience, including upgraded seating, state-of-the-art sound and lighting systems, and improved concessions. Whether you're cheering on the Cyclones or singing along with your favorite band, the Heritage Bank Center is a must-visit destination in the city.
